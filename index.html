Why engineering?

I am a computer engineer currently working on finishing my degree at Texas A&M university. I have been enrolled since fall of 2018 and am currently in my junior year by hours.When I was younger, I was not sure exactly what I wanted to do other than I wanted something engaging and challenging. Whenever I was introduced to engineering, it immediately caught my attention due to the problem solving and opportunity to be challenged all the time. I enjoy engineering because of the challenges and excitement it brings to life. Each day, while you may have a certain routine you follow, chances are you won't experience the same problems day by day as you might with other jobs. Every day brings new challenges and new opportunities to learn.

About me:

Besides being a full time student here at TAMU, I have had work experience as a Lifeguard and a Head lifeguard. Both of these positions involved teamwork and a great deal of team building to establish an understanding between the members of what each person should be doing in x, y or z scenarios. For example: if a child has had a cut, or bug sting, one or more of the lifeguards will attend to the child while the other/s go to find the child's guardian. Both positions offer some degree of patron or customer interaction, however being a head lifeguard significantly increased the number of interactions I would have with the guests at the pool. I would often be the one to receive compliments or questions about the facility, or have to deal with problems that people had with the facility or any situations that came up at the pool. These could include things as simple as giving someone directions or asking them to be a bit less rowdy, to asking a family to leave for any extraordinary circumstances. My experience as a guard and headguard have helped me develop great communication and teamwork skills.

Outside of work or school related things, I try to be as active and physical as I can, going to the gym and playing sports with friends. I have also started teaching myself to play guitar on the side because only playing video games during free time can be fun, but not super engaging.
